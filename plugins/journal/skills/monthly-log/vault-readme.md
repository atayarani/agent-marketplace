# Monthly journal

Per-month capture pages, one file per month at `YYYY-MM.md`, written into across the month as the 4 priorities advance and reflection notes accumulate. Shape follows the BuJo Rapid Logging conventions from Ryder Carroll's *How to Bullet Journal: Daily vs. Weekly vs. Monthly* and *Bullet Journal in 5 Minutes a Day for Busy People*. Same signifier vocabulary as the daily and weekly cadences — see [[notes/journal/daily/README|the daily journal README]] for the canonical convention reference.

## The monthly action plan

Carroll's distinctive monthly-cadence shape is the **4-priority action plan**:

1. **List**: brainstorm what you want to act on this month as un-numbered `•` action lines.
2. **Pick 4**: identify the 4 most important.
3. **Number 1-4 by importance**: prefix the chosen actions with `1.` through `4.`. The number captures relative importance, not deadlines.

Carroll's load-bearing constraint: 4 is the cap. *"These are the four things that would make this month a win. This also gives you one priority a week for the rest of the month."* (~4 priorities × ~4 weeks ≈ informal one-per-week.)

Unchosen `•` actions stay un-numbered below — available context (you might still act on them) but not the month's commitment.

## Monthly reflection (Carroll-attested ritual)

At the start of each month, walk the **previous** monthly entry's 4 priorities and state-mark each:

- `✕` — completed
- `→` — migrated to this month (re-write as a fresh un-numbered `•` candidate in this month's entry)
- `»` — exported (moved to a digital task manager, calendar, etc.)
- `~~1. • action~~` — dropped, no longer relevant

Then capture any reflection notes about the month gone by (themes, mood shifts, what worked, what didn't) as ordinary signified entries (`–` notes, `=` moods) on the previous month's entry.

## Stacking with weekly and daily

The monthly entry is the **zoom-out anchor** for the shorter cadences:

- This month's 4 priorities should be visible from the [[notes/journal/weekly|weekly]] action plan (each week's plan should advance one or more of the month's priorities).
- This month's 4 priorities should also surface in the [[notes/journal/daily|daily]] AM ritual (via the `daily-log` skill's cross-cadence read).

The cadences are designed to stack — same template shape across daily/weekly/monthly, so you can zoom in and out without context-switching.

## Chapter-level intentions (separate, optional)

If a longer-horizon intention shape is wanted, the Carroll-attested home for it is **chapter-level intentions on a labeled page at the start of a new notebook** — distinct from monthly priorities; they don't share an artifact.
